Hearing Assessments

  • Hearing assessments are in-office evaluations that measure how well you hear sounds and understand speech.
  • A clear hearing test result shows the type and degree of hearing loss, so your audiologist can build the right plan for daily life.
  • New Leaf Hearing Care offers personalized hearing tests in Arvada, CO and Littleton, CO.

Hearing tests or hearing assessments check how well you hear, detect sounds that you might miss, and where you struggle most.

Signs It’s Time to Schedule

Many people wait because they “get by.” That delay can lead to more stress and less connection.

Schedule a hearing test if you notice these changes:

You ask people to repeat words.

Voices sound soft or unclear.

Group talk feels hard to follow.

You turn the TV up higher than others want.

You feel tired after long talks.

You avoid restaurants because noise takes over.

What Hearing Tests Check

A hearing test is not one quick screen. A complete visit checks more than volume.

Your audiologist measures:

  • How soft a sound can be before you hear it.
  • How clear speech sounds to you.
  • Which pitches are harder for you.
  • How each ear works on its own.
  • How your ears handle sound in quiet settings.

These results guide the next step. You leave with a clear direction.

What Happens During Your Visit

Your appointment starts with a short talk. We ask what you hear, what you miss, and what settings feel hardest.

We also review health history. This includes noise exposure, ear problems, and past hearing care.

Next, we look in your ears. This check helps spot issues that block sound, like wax.

Then we complete hearing tests in a quiet space. You respond to tones and speech.

Last, we review your results in plain words. You get time to ask questions, so you feel sure about the plan.

Why an Audiologist Matters

Phone apps and online screens do not give the full picture. They cannot check your ears. They do not give a clinical result you can trust.

An audiologist can also spot warning signs that need medical care.

If you want a real answer, schedule hearing tests with a licensed team.

What Your Results Can Reveal

Hearing tests can show three key details.

Level of hearing loss

+

Your results show how much sound you need to hear speech and everyday noise.

Type of hearing loss

+

Your results can point to where the problem starts. That detail helps guide treatment.

Next step that fits your life

+

Your plan can include hearing aids, assistive devices, custom hearing protection, or training that improves how you follow speech.

If tinnitus is part of your concern, we can add tinnitus-focused testing and counseling.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a hearing test?

+

A hearing test is an in-office exam that measures what sounds you hear and how well you understand speech, then turns those results into a clear care plan.

How does a hearing test work at New Leaf Hearing Care?

+

Your visit includes a health review, an ear check, hearing testing in a quiet room, and a results review with an audiologist who explains what the findings mean.

How do I know which hearing test I need?

+

If you want a basic check, a Basic Hearing Exam fits that goal. If you have trouble in daily talks and want treatment options, a Functional Hearing Assessment gives a deeper review.
