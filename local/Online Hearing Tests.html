Online Hearing Tests

  • Online hearing tests are quick at-home screenings that give a rough idea of your hearing ability using a phone or computer.
  • Results can point to a problem, but only an audiologist can confirm the cause and build a care plan you can trust.
  • New Leaf Hearing Care helps patients after online hearing tests with professional hearing care in Arvada, CO and Littleton, CO.

Online hearing tests are easy to access. Many people take one after they notice the TV getting louder, speech sounding unclear, or noise making conversations hard.

These tools can be a helpful first step. Online hearing tests can also create false worry or false relief, depending on the device, the room, and the volume settings.

New Leaf Hearing Care helps you move from a quick screen to clear answers. Hearing tests and audiologist consultations are available in Arvada, CO and Littleton, CO (formerly Columbine Hearing Care).

What Online Hearing Tests Can Do Well

Online tests work best as a simple check. They can help you notice a possible issue sooner.

A good online test can show that you miss certain tones. It can also highlight trouble with speech sounds.

That signal can push you to take the next step. For many people, that next step is the first real hearing exam they have had in years.

Why Online Results Can Be Wrong

Online tests are not done in a controlled setting. Sound can change from one phone to the next.

Room noise changes results fast.

A fan, air vent, traffic, or a nearby TV can hide test tones.

Headphones also matter. Cheap earbuds and loose-fitting headphones can change what you hear during the test.

Volume settings create another problem. Too high or too low can make the result meaningless.

What Online Hearing Tests Cannot Measure

Hearing is more than “can you hear a beep.” A full hearing evaluation checks the whole system.

Online tests do not look in your ears.

  • Wax buildup can block sound and mimic the feeling of hearing loss.

Online tests do not check middle-ear function.

  • Fluid or pressure problems can affect hearing and comfort.

Online tests do not measure speech understanding in the same way a clinic can.

  • Many people struggle most with speech, not tones.

Online tests also do not help rule out medical red flags.

  • Sudden hearing loss, one-sided changes, pain, drainage, or dizziness need prompt care.

A Helpful Way to Use Online Tests

Online tests are useful when you treat them like a screen. They can help you decide if it is time to get checked.

If your result shows concern, book an in-person exam. If your result looks normal but daily life still feels hard, book an exam anyway.

Your experience matters more than a score. People live with hearing trouble for years while still “passing” quick screens.

Signs You Should See an Audiologist

A hearing exam is the right move when hearing everyday sounds or conversations are getting harder.

You should see an audiologist if:

  • Speech sounds unclear in restaurants or group dinners.
  • You rely on captions more than you used to.
  • You feel tired after conversations.
  • You miss doorbells, timers, or soft voices.
  • Family members say you misunderstand them.
  • Ringing or buzzing is present, especially at night.

These signs point to a need for real testing and real guidance.

What an In-Person Hearing Test Includes

At New Leaf Hearing Care, a hearing evaluation starts with a conversation. We want to know what you hear, what you miss, and where you struggle most.

Next comes an ear inspection (otoscopy). This helps identify wax and other issues that can block sound.

We then complete a full hearing test with professional equipment. Speech testing is included, because understanding words is the goal.

You leave with results explained properly and a plan that fits your needs.

What Your Results Can Tell You

A clinical test shows more than “yes or no.” It shows patterns that guide smart care.

Testing can identify which pitches you miss. That matters because speech is made of many small sounds.

Results can also show if one ear is different. Asymmetry can change treatment and referral decisions.

Your audiologist can then connect the results to your life. That means specific guidance for work calls, restaurants, family time, and social events.

What Happens After Hearing Testing

Some patients need simple care first. Wax removal can restore hearing quickly when wax is the cause.

Other patients need a hearing wellness plan. That may include hearing aids, assistive listening devices, or training tools that build listening skills.

If tinnitus is part of the picture, tinnitus counseling and sound support can be added to the plan.

The goal stays the same. Better communication and less effort during everyday life.

Why Local Audiology Care Makes a Difference

Online tests cannot adjust to your needs, but a clinic visit can.

An audiologist takes time to listen. Your care plan is built around your goals, not a generic result page.

Follow-up matters too. Hearing changes over time, and your plan should keep up.

That long-term relationship is what helps people stay confident and engaged.

FAQs

How do I know if my online hearing test result is accurate?

+

Accuracy depends on a quiet room, good headphones, correct volume, and a calibrated device, so the only way to confirm the result is an in-person hearing evaluation with an audiologist.

How does an in-person hearing test compare to an online test?

+

An in-person test checks your ears, measures hearing with calibrated equipment, tests speech understanding, and provides a clear diagnosis and treatment plan based on your needs.

How do I schedule a hearing test after taking an online test?

+

You can schedule a hearing exam with New Leaf Hearing Care in Arvada or Littleton by calling the office or booking online, and the team will guide you to the right appointment type.
