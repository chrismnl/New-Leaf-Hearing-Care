Start With a Hearing Test You Can Trust

A hearing aid should never be picked from a chart or a website quiz. The right fit begins with accurate testing done by an audiologist.

Your hearing test shows what sounds you miss and what speech cues are hardest to catch. It also shows how each ear works on its own.

That information becomes your roadmap. It keeps the next step focused and prevents guesswork.

What You Want Your Hearing Aids to Do

A “good” hearing aid is not the same for everyone. The best choice is the one that supports your real life.

We ask questions like:

Which rooms feel hardest, and why?

Do you struggle most in noise or one-on-one?

Do you take phone calls for work or family?

Do you want rechargeable power?

Do you wear glasses, masks, or helmets often?

Those details shape the recommendation. This is hearing wellness, not a quick sale.

Hearing Aid Styles Explained in Plain Terms

Hearing aids come in a few main styles. Each style has strengths that fit certain ears and needs.

Behind-the-ear (BTE)

+

This style sits behind the ear with a piece that goes into the ear canal. It works well for many hearing levels and can feel stable during active days.

Receiver-in-canal (RIC)

+

This style sits behind the ear with a small wire to the ear canal. Many people like the comfort and the natural feel.

In-the-ear (ITE)

+

This style fits inside the outer ear. It can be easier to handle for some patients who want a single piece.

In-the-canal (ITC) and completely-in-canal (CIC)

+

These fit deeper in the ear canal and are less visible. Fit depends on ear shape, and handling can be harder for some hands.

Style choice depends on your hearing needs, your ear shape, and how you use your hands each day. Our audiologists at New Leaf Hearing Care will help you pick what works, not what looks good on a shelf.

Features That Make a Real Difference

Some features sound impressive, but do not help every patient. We focus on the ones that change daily listening.

Speech help in noise

+

Advanced microphones and noise controls help you follow speech in busy places. This matters for restaurants, events, and family gatherings.

Bluetooth phone and media

+

Many hearing aids can stream calls, music, and videos. This can make calls clearer and reduce the need to hold the phone in one “sweet spot.”

Rechargeable vs. disposable batteries

+

Rechargeable models reduce battery changes and can be simpler day to day. Disposable batteries work well for people who want quick swaps and longer storage.

Tinnitus support

+

Some devices include sound options that can help tinnitus feel less dominant. Your audiologist can pair this with counseling and a plan you can follow.

Keep in mind that hearing aid features should fit your life. A device packed with extras can still feel like the wrong choice if it does not match your needs.

Comfort and Fit Matter as Much as Sound

A hearing aid has to feel right, or it will sit in a drawer.

Comfort depends on shape, size, and how it seals in the ear.

Some patients do well with soft tips. Others need custom earmolds for a secure feel and steady sound.

Your provider checks how your hearing aids fit at every visit.

Why Programming and Verification Matter

Two people can wear the same model and have very different results. Programming is what makes a hearing aid match your hearing.

At New Leaf Hearing Care, we adjust settings based on your test and your feedback. We also use Real Ear Measurements, which check how the hearing aid performs in your ear.

This step matters because every ear shapes sound in a unique way. Verification confirms the device is doing what it should for you.

Your First Weeks Set the Tone

Your brain needs time to adjust to clearer sound. That is normal and expected.

We guide you through the early weeks with follow-up visits and fine-tuning. You learn how to wear the devices, how to care for them, and how to handle common listening challenges.

That support is part of the service, not an add-on.

FAQs

What is choosing a hearing aid based on?

+

Choosing a hearing aid is based on your hearing test results, your ear shape, your comfort needs, and the places where you listen every day, like work, home, and restaurants.

How do I choose the right hearing aid style?

+

Your audiologist helps you choose the right style by checking your hearing level, looking at your ear canal size, and matching the device to how you use your hands, glasses, and masks.

How does a hearing aid fitting work?

+

A fitting includes selecting the right size, checking comfort, programming the hearing aids to match your hearing test, and teaching you how to use and clean them, followed by fine-tuning visits.

What is Real Ear Measurement and why does it matter?

+

Real Ear Measurement is an in-office check that measures how the hearing aid sounds in your ear, so the settings can be verified and adjusted for accurate speech clarity.
